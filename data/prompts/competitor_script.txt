## Translation Rules

1. **Preserve the topic.** If the original is about AI agents monitoring Reddit, the rewrite is about AI agents monitoring Reddit. Do not pivot to a different subject.

2. **Preserve the hook mechanic.** If the original opens with a proof-first demo, your rewrite opens with a proof-first demo. If the original opens with a contrarian statement, your rewrite opens with a contrarian statement.

3. **Preserve the structural beats.** Identify the original's structure (e.g. hook → problem → demo → resolution → CTA) and replicate that sequence.

4. **Preserve the CTA's *effect*, not its mechanism — and never copy an unscalable CTA.**
   - Identify what the original CTA is trying to achieve: engagement (comments), saves, shares, follows, community-drive, or a lead-magnet handoff.
   - If the effect is engagement / save / share / follow, preserve that effect in the creator's voice.
   - **If the original's mechanism is a lead-magnet handoff — e.g. "comment X and I'll DM you Y," "drop KEYWORD and I'll send the template," anything that requires the creator to respond individually — DO NOT COPY IT.** Substitute with a scalable alternative (opinion prompt, discussion question, save ask, share ask, follow ask, contrarian challenge) and flag the substitution in Notes naming it as an "unscalable CTA substitution."
   - **Never reference an artifact the creator hasn't declared.** Do not mention "my course," "my newsletter," "my template," "the link in bio," or any specific asset unless the creator's style profile explicitly lists that asset as something the creator promotes. If the original drives to the competitor's owned asset, substitute with a scalable alternative rather than pretending the creator has an equivalent.
   - If the creator's style profile lists a giveaway-style CTA as a "proven pattern" but the rest of the profile (or the lazy defaults) signals a pivot away from that content era, prefer a scalable alternative and flag it in Notes.

5. **Apply the creator's Voice.** Every line must read like the creator would deliver it. Use their vocabulary register, their signature phrases where natural, their cadence markers, their colloquialisms. If the creator is British and uses "rubbish," use "rubbish," not "nonsense."

6. **Apply Proven Patterns where the structure allows.** If the creator's profile lists a proven pattern that the original already uses, double down on it. Lean into mechanics the creator has verifiable evidence for.

7. **Actively REFUSE anti-patterns.** Scan the creator's Anti-Patterns section. If the original uses a pattern listed there (e.g. `passive_series_continuation`, `slow_conversational_preamble`, `abrupt_ending_no_cta`), REPLACE that beat with a compliant alternative drawn from the Proven Patterns section. You MUST flag this substitution in the output `Notes` section — naming both the anti-pattern and the replacement.

8. **Compress, don't extend.** If the original is 3 minutes and the creator's profile favours 45–90 second bites, compress. Never pad a short script just because the original was short.

9. **Stage directions must respect the Production Style above.** Do not invent shots, cutaways, or effects the creator does not use. If in doubt, fewer directions beats more.

10. **Never open with "In this video," "Today I'm going to talk about," "Hey guys," or any greeting / throat-clearing.** These are default LLM filler and violate the 2-second hook rule regardless of whether the creator's profile mentions them.
