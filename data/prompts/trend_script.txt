## Fact-Check Discipline

The Research Brief above is the authoritative source. It was produced with live Google Search grounding, so it has been fact-checked at the research stage.

Your job in this step is to apply the creator's voice to facts that are **already in the research**. You must NOT introduce new facts that aren't in the research.

Rules:
1. **Every specific factual claim in every script must be traceable to the research brief.** Company names, product names, people, dollar amounts, percentages, dates, launches, pricing, benchmarks, statistics — all must appear in the research. If it's not in the research, it doesn't go in the script.
2. **If a topic idea from the research is strong but lacks a specific detail you'd like to use, either (a) rephrase vaguely or (b) drop that detail.** Example: research mentions "Cursor's cloud agents launched recently" without a date → your script says "Cursor just launched cloud agents," NOT "Cursor launched cloud agents on February 14."
3. **Cite sources per script.** Every significant factual claim gets a citation in the `sources` array pointing back to the research brief's section (and the URL the research cited, if present).
4. **Never fabricate a statistic, quote, date, or figure.** If the research doesn't state it, it doesn't exist for the purposes of this script.
5. **Creator-voice statements (opinions, hot takes) are fine without citations.** Factual assertions require them.

## Task

Generate **{script_count}** distinct scripts. Each must be ready to film with minimal further editing. Spread across the research's opportunities — do NOT generate multiple variants of the same topic.

### Selection Rules
- Pick topics from the research's Script Opportunity Ranking if available; otherwise from the Trending Tools, Active Debates, Pain Points, or Emerging Use Cases sections.
- Prefer topics with short shelf life (timely) over evergreen ones — this is a trend generator, not a library.
- Prefer topics where the research cited a specific primary source over vaguer items.
- Never use topics the research lists under Dead / Stale Topics.

### Writing Rules (apply to every script)
1. **Hook in 0–3 seconds.** Use a hook pattern from the creator's Proven Patterns section. Match the hook mechanic to the research angle (news_react → react to the specific launch, hot_take → contrarian statement, warning → risk in first sentence, etc.).
2. **Apply the creator's voice throughout.** Vocabulary register, signature phrases, colloquialisms, cadence markers.
3. **Refuse anti-patterns.** If tempted to use one, substitute with a proven pattern and flag it in `notes`.
4. **CTA is non-negotiable.** Every script ends with a one-line call to action in the creator's CTA style.
5. **CTAs must be scalable — no manual follow-up promises.** The CTA must be deliverable to every viewer at zero marginal effort from the creator.
   - **FORBIDDEN:** "Comment X and I'll DM you Y," "Comment X and I'll send you the template / guide / breakdown," "Drop 'KEYWORD' and I'll reply with the link," or any other promise that requires the creator to respond to individual viewers.
   - **FORBIDDEN:** references to artifacts the creator has not declared ("my course", "my newsletter", "my template", "the link in bio") UNLESS the style profile explicitly lists that artifact as something the creator uses.
   - **ALLOWED:** opinion prompts ("Agree? Disagree?"), discussion questions ("Which are you using?"), contrarian challenges ("Change my mind"), follow asks ("Follow for more on X"), save asks ("Save this for when you debug X"), share asks ("Send this to someone stuck on X"), or a single declarative closing statement that doesn't ask for anything.
   - If the creator's style profile lists a giveaway-style CTA as a "proven pattern" but also shows signs the creator has pivoted away from it, prefer a scalable alternative and flag the substitution in `notes`.
6. **Respect the production style block above.** No stage directions outside the allowed set.
7. **Never open with "In this video," "Today I'm going to," "Hey guys," or any greeting / throat-clearing.**
8. **Match length to format tag.** `bite` = 45–90s (≈120–225 words), `demo` = 60–120s (≈150–300 words), `news_react` = 30–90s (≈75–225 words), `long_form` = 2–4 min (≈300–750 words).
